# Kapia, the Northern Continent

In the North of Propiti lies Kapia, a continent comprised of Humans, Elves, Dwarves, Leonin, and Dragonborn, though other races abound as well. After the assault on Oniphia by the Kingdom of Einron, Kingdom of Thenadel, and the Rising Storm, the Council of Sovereigns collapsed and the alliance between the kingdoms ended. Now, border skirmishes arise, trade disputes occur, and the citizens of the kingdom sit with baited breath as the threat of war between the kingdoms looms.

## The Red Wood Forest

To the West of the Silver Summit mountain range sits the Red Wood Forest, the home of the Elves in Kapia. It has two major cities, Yanashara and Thenadel, with Thenadel serving as the capital. After the aid of Del Ragon and the other members of the High Five, the World Tree was burned, a new one was planted in its place, and the Elves began cleansing the forest of the spreading corruption caused during the Artificer's War.

After the war, the Elves opened up their forest to the Shadar-Kai, the elves that had been affected by the Dracolich's dark magic and who had hid themselves away in the Shadowlands, the realm between life and death. The Shadar-Kai have been doing their part to aid in the cleansing of the forest, as well as the damage that the temporal magic wrought on the forest from the Jax duplicate.

Though the Elves were allied with the Dwarvers of the Thundercall Mountains during the Artificer's War, age-old grudges have been brought to light again, and war threatens to break out between the Elves and the Dwarves. Trade agreements, border disputes, and ancient wrongs could bring the two regions to blow once again, even though both regions had just gotten through a war not long ago.

## Thundercall Mountains

The Dwarves Thundercall Mountain were the first to lead the charge against the Prime Sovereign and the capital after they were attacked by the Kapian army for harboring the leadership of the Rising Storm. Thanks to the aid of High Five, the Dwarves were able to fight off the invasion and contact the Elves of the Red Wood Forest to form an alliance against the capital.

They also lost their Sovereign during the attack, leading to the Sovereign's daughter taking up the mantle of leadership for the Dwarves. Since the end of the Artificer's War, she's been attempting to rebuild the region. Strengthening the various mining operations in the region, investing more into the science of artificery, and adding members to the ranks of their new military. While losing their old Sovereign threatened the stability of the region, the new Sovereign has stepped up and won the admiration of the Dwarves for her actions.

Many of the older Dwarves have begun stoking old grudges between Dwarven clans and Elvish families, causing rifts between the two regions even so shortly after their alliance against the capital. Both side view themselves as the reason behind the success of the fall of the capital, and expect concessions to be given based on that belief. Border disputes, trade agreements, and even common courtesy are going by the wayside as both regions seem to be gearing up for conflict.

## Stonemantle

Formerly known as the Strength of Kapia, the Leonin of Stonemantle proved to be the greatest warriors that the Kapian army fielded during the Artificer's War, and would have posed a problem to the alliance of Elves and Dwarves if not for the sudden passing of their Sovereign at the start of the rebellion. After their passing, the ruling Leonin ended up in a deadlock in choosing their next Sovereign, keeping them out of the fight.

Even years after the end of the war, the Leonin have still not been able to select a replacement for their previous leader, leaving the borders of Stonemantle threatened; while the Dwarves are currently dealing with their situation with the Elves, the Glittering Coasts does border Stonemantle, and having been scorned by the other regions due to staying allied with the Prime Sovereign during the war, are using this as an opportunity to expand the borders of the Glittering Coast into Stonemantle.



## The Glittering Coast

Along Kapia’s southeastern coastline lie the Gem Beaches, named for the brilliant crystalline sands and colorful mineral formations that shimmer beneath the sun and moon alike. Dragonborn clans dominate the region, building vibrant coastal cities into cliffsides, reefs, and volcanic outcroppings.

The dragonborn of the Gem Beaches maintain strong ancestral ties to dragonkind, though opinions differ greatly regarding the legacy of true dragons. Some clans revere them as divine ancestors, while others remember only destruction and tyranny.

Trade flourishes throughout the region, as merchants from across Propiti seek rare gems, enchanted pearls, and volcanic metals harvested from beneath the sea cliffs.

## The Crown’s Peninsula

Stretching southward into the surrounding seas, the Crown’s Peninsula serves as the political and economic heart of Kapia. Human kingdoms, noble houses, and sprawling cities dominate the region, connected by extensive roads, ports, and fortresses.

While many rulers claim civilization thrives strongest upon the peninsula, its history is one of constant political struggle. Alliances shift rapidly between noble families, merchant guilds, and military orders. Yet despite internal rivalries, the peninsula often stands united against threats emerging from the rest of the continent.

Its capital cities contain some of the greatest universities, magical academies, and religious institutions in Propiti, drawing scholars and adventurers from every corner of the world.

## The White Sand Desert

West of the peninsula stretches the White Sand Desert, a haunting expanse of pale dunes that gleam like snow beneath the sunlight. Violent winds constantly reshape the landscape, uncovering ancient ruins one day only to bury them again the next.

At the desert’s center stands the White Flame Monastery, an isolated sanctuary devoted to discipline, enlightenment, and mastery over body and spirit. Monks from across Kapia journey into the desert seeking wisdom, though many never return. The monastery’s teachings are mysterious, and rumors persist that its masters guard knowledge capable of altering fate itself.

Strange phenomena plague the desert: ghostly lights drifting across dunes at night, ruins that appear only during sandstorms, and whispers carried on winds that seem almost alive.

## Death's Point

No region in Kapia inspires greater fear than the Dead Coast.

Long ago, this barren shoreline became the final battlefield in the war against Tenebris, the dreaded dracolich whose armies nearly plunged the continent into ruin. Though Tenebris was ultimately defeated, the battle poisoned the land itself. The coast remains scarred by necromantic corruption centuries later.

Blackened cliffs rise above dead waters where no fish swim. Ruined fortresses and shattered siege engines still litter the shoreline, half-buried beneath ash and salt. Undead creatures wander the region endlessly, animated by lingering magic that refuses to fade.

Even now, many believe Tenebris is not truly gone.

Ships avoid the waters near the Dead Coast whenever possible, and those who travel there often speak of distant wingbeats echoing through the fog long after sunset.

## A Continent of Ancient Fate

Kapia is a land balanced between civilization and catastrophe, where ancient histories still shape the present day. Every kingdom carries old grudges, every ruin hides forgotten truths, and every region bears the marks of powers far older than mortal memory.

For adventurers, scholars, and conquerors alike, Kapia offers opportunity beyond measure—but also dangers capable of consuming entire nations.
