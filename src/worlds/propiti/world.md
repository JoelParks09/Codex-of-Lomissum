# Propiti, Broken by Conflict

The world of Propiti is a realm still bleeding from the wounds of the Artificer’s War, a catastrophic conflict that reshaped nations, shattered alliances, and forever altered the balance of power across the planet. Once a world of flourishing kingdoms, ancient traditions, and uneasy political order, Propiti now stands divided—its people struggling to rebuild amidst the ruins left behind by ambition, arcane experimentation, and mechanized warfare.

At the center of the conflict stood the Seong Company, an organization whose influence spread across both governments and battlefields alike. Through their mastery of artificery, forbidden experimentation, and the creation of the construct armies, the Seong Company transformed war into something the world had never before witnessed. Entire cities vanished beneath magical bombardments, landscapes were twisted by unstable arcane energy, and nations that once stood united fractured under the weight of betrayal and fear.

Though the war has officially ended, peace remains fragile.

The continents of Kapia and Iucall—once united under centralized powers during the conflict—have splintered into independent kingdoms, fractured alliances, and bitter rivalries. Old enemies who fought side-by-side now argue over borders, trade, resources, and political influence. Small wars and border skirmishes erupt constantly, while rulers, generals, guilds, and revolutionaries attempt to secure power in an age where no true authority remains.

Across taverns, courts, monasteries, and battlefields alike, one truth is universally understood: the world survived the Artificer’s War, but it has not yet survived its consequences.

### Kapia, the Northern Continent

To the north lies the continent of Kapia, once governed beneath the banner of the Prime Sovereign and the Council of Sovereigns. For generations, the council maintained order between the continent’s many races and kingdoms, balancing fragile alliances through diplomacy, military power, and the technological influence of the Seong Company.

That order collapsed during the assault on the capital city of Oniphia.

The fall of the city marked the end of both the Prime Sovereign’s reign and the Seong Company’s political dominance in Kapia. In the aftermath, long-suppressed tensions resurfaced almost immediately, plunging the continent into instability. Noble houses fractured, trade routes became contested, and ancient rivalries once buried beneath wartime unity began to emerge once more.

The dwarves and elves—historic enemies who had reluctantly united during the war—now find themselves locked in increasingly hostile trade disputes and political disagreements. While open war has not yet erupted between them, many fear it is only a matter of time before old grudges overcome recent cooperation.

Human territories remain in chaos following the destruction of centralized rule. Competing noble factions, military remnants, merchant alliances, and revolutionary groups all vie for control of what was once the political heart of Kapia. Some seek to restore the old order, while others believe the continent must abandon monarchy entirely if it hopes to survive.

The dragonborn suffer perhaps the harshest consequences of all. As the staunchest supporters of the Prime Sovereign during the war, they are viewed with deep suspicion by much of the continent. Though many dragonborn argue they fought for stability rather than tyranny, resentment toward them continues to grow. Trade sanctions, political isolation, and public hostility have transformed them into near-pariahs among their former allies.

Meanwhile, the Leonin kingdoms have turned inward following the death of their sovereign. Once among the most unified military powers in Kapia, the Leonin now struggle with internal divisions regarding leadership, tradition, and their role in the continent’s uncertain future. Some clans advocate isolation, believing Kapia’s politics to be beyond saving, while others argue that abandoning the continent now will only guarantee greater wars to come.

Despite its instability, Kapia remains one of the most powerful regions in Propiti—a continent rich with resources, ancient magic, and ambitious rulers all attempting to shape what comes next.

### Iucall, the Southern Continent

To the south lies Iucall, a continent forever changed by rebellion.

Unlike Kapia, which fractured after the fall of its rulers, Iucall’s transformation came through revolution. The continent was once ruled by the King’s Congress under the authority of the Royal King, whose regime maintained control through military power, political manipulation, and close ties with the Seong Company. While many regions prospered under the Congress, countless others suffered beneath corruption, exploitation, and authoritarian rule.

That rule came to an end during the final years of the Artificer’s War.

Led primarily by gnomish strategists and half-orc warlords, rebel forces launched a massive campaign against the capital city of Clona. Against overwhelming odds, the revolution succeeded in overthrowing the Royal King’s forces and dismantling the Seong Company’s influence across much of the continent.

In the years since, the alliance between the gnomes and the newly crowned half-orc king has become the strongest political force in Iucall. Together, they have attempted to rebuild the continent and establish a more stable government, though the scars of war continue to complicate those efforts.

Nowhere is this struggle more visible than in Clona itself.

Though reconstruction efforts continue, the city remains plagued by organized crime, corruption, political extremism, and opportunistic warlords seeking to seize control of the weakened capital. Entire districts remain half-destroyed, while competing factions attempt to shape what the future government of Iucall will become.

Further east, the Tabaxi-controlled city of Belhan occupies one of the most important trade positions on the continent. Though the Tabaxi once maintained strong ties with the Royal King, they have since attempted to distance themselves from the failures of the old regime. Even so, distrust toward them remains widespread among many of the continent’s newer powers.

Despite this tension, Belhan’s importance to continental trade has forced many regions to cooperate with the city whether they trust it or not. Merchant fleets, smugglers, diplomats, and mercenary companies all pass through its ports, making it one of the busiest—and most dangerous—cities in southern Propiti.

To the southeast, the Yuan-ti kingdoms remain largely isolated within the dense jungles they call home. Unlike the other powers of Iucall, the Yuan-ti have deliberately avoided deep involvement in continental politics following the war. Their rulers maintain strict borders and rarely interfere with outside affairs, leading many to speculate about what truly occurs within the jungles beyond their territory.

Some believe the Yuan-ti simply wish to avoid another devastating war.

Others believe they are preparing for something far greater.

### Votdare, the Shattered Island

Between Kapia and Iucall lies the shattered archipelago of Votdare, the cursed heart of the Artificer’s War.

Once used as the primary staging ground for the Seong Company’s experiments, research facilities, and military production, Votdare became synonymous with the horrors unleashed during the conflict. Here, artificers and war mages pushed beyond ethical boundaries in pursuit of power, creating weapons, constructs, and magical phenomena that even now remain poorly understood.

When the war ended, Votdare did not recover.

The islands remain saturated with unstable magic and lingering corruption. Entire regions of the archipelago have been twisted beyond recognition, their landscapes warped by failed experiments and catastrophic magical detonations. Monsters roam freely across the islands, many mutated by arcane contamination or created directly within abandoned Seong laboratories buried beneath the earth.

Ruined fortresses, shattered construct foundries, underground research vaults, and forgotten testing facilities still lie hidden across Votdare, sealed away by governments fearful of what might still remain inside.

In the aftermath of the war, both Kapia and Iucall agreed to declare the archipelago off-limits to ordinary citizens. Naval patrols enforce strict quarantines around the islands, and only sanctioned military, research, or recovery expeditions are permitted to enter.

Even then, many never return.

Rumors persist that rogue artificers, scavengers, cultists, and surviving constructs still inhabit the ruins of Votdare. Others whisper that some of the Seong Company’s greatest creations were never destroyed at all—and may still be waiting beneath the islands for someone foolish enough to awaken them.
